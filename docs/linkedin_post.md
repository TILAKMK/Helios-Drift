# LINKEDIN ANNOUNCEMENT DRAFT

🚨 Exciting news! We have just open-sourced **Phantom Protocol** (under the Helios-Drift repository) — a zero-hardware early-warning smart-city safety framework that reads the room before disaster strikes.

Traditional warning grids are reactive: they wait for single physical sensors (seismic, heat, smoke) to cross red lines. By then, it’s already too late.

Phantom Protocol takes a different approach. It fuses multiple commodity channels (ambient noise floors, multi-path Wi-Fi variance, and accelerometer micro-deviations) to compute a unified environmental hazard score.

### Key Highlights:
1. **Adaptive Baseline Engines**: Tracks diurnal baseline fluctuations and filters out localized sensor calibration drift to keep false positives <1%.
2. **Spatial Graph Fusion**: Propagates danger indices along topological transit nodes.
3. **Double-Head LSTM Predictor**: Forecasts hazard events and estimates lead times in real-time.
4. **React Native Mobile Telemetry Client**: Streamlines commodity edge telemetry collection.

In our campus validations, the protocol predicted simulated hazard arrivals with an average **lead time error under 4.3 minutes**.
